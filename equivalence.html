Equivalence¶

As before, load the query results:

from example_data import results

Different Object instances may be used in different datasets which in fact refer to the same type of thing.

Fig. 8 Initial observations for Crushed stone, Crushed stone in PRODCOM, and Crushed stone in BGS.¶

To allow querying of observations in an uniform way, we want to propagate the observation to all the equivalent objects.

First, this is an equivalence relation (it is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive).

Fig. 9 Extension of the equivalence relation.¶

And we should also consider the observations inferred by composition.

Fig. 10 Direct and inferred observations for Crushed stone, Crushed stone in PRODCOM, and Crushed stone in BGS.¶

We want to propagate observations among all the equivalent objects avoiding duplication.

PREFIX : <https://ukfires.org/probs/ontology/>

SELECT DISTINCT ?Observation ?EObjectName
WHERE {
    ?Object :objectName "Crushed stone" ;
            :objectEquivalentTo ?EObject .
  	?EObject :objectName ?EObjectName .
    ?Observation :objectDefinedBy ?EObject .
}
ORDER BY ?EObjectName ?Observation
